Part 1: U.S. Citizen Petitioner Documents

Identity & Citizenship

  • Proof of U.S. citizenship (passport, birth certificate, naturalization certificate, or consular report of birth abroad)
  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or state ID)
  • Name change documentation (if applicable — marriage certificate, court order)

Marital History

  • Termination proof for ALL prior marriages (divorce decrees, death certificates, annulments)
Pro tip: You need proof that EVERY previous marriage ended, not just the most recent one. Missing one is a top reason for RFEs.

Financial

  • Most recent federal tax return (Form 1040) or IRS tax transcript
  • Recent pay stubs (2-3 months)
  • Employment verification letter
  • If self-employed: business license, profit/loss statement, 1099s
  • If using joint sponsor: their tax returns, pay stubs, and I-134

Part 2: Foreign Fiancé(e) Documents

Identity

  • Valid passport (copy of photo page — must be valid 6+ months beyond planned entry)
  • Birth certificate (original or certified copy)
  • 2 passport-style photos (2x2 inches, white background, within 6 months)

Marital History

  • Termination proof for ALL prior marriages
  • Single status certificate / Certificate of No Marriage (required by some countries)

Criminal/Background

  • Police clearance from each country lived in 6+ months after age 16
Philippines: NBI clearance (allow 2-4 weeks)
Colombia: Certificado de Antecedentes Judiciales
Mexico: Constancia de No Antecedentes Penales
India: Police Clearance Certificate from Passport Office

Part 3: Relationship Evidence (CRITICAL)

This is the #1 reason for RFEs. 30% of K-1 petitions get an RFE in 2026, and weak relationship evidence is the top trigger. More is better.

Meeting Requirement

  • Proof of in-person meeting within past 2 years (passport stamps, airline tickets, hotel reservations, photos with dates)

Communication Evidence

  • Call logs (phone records showing frequency and duration)
  • Messaging screenshots (WhatsApp, Messenger, LINE — show range of dates)
  • Video call logs (Skype, Zoom, FaceTime screenshots)
  • Letters, cards, or gift receipts
  • Social media interactions (tagged photos, comments, relationship status)
Pro tip: Show CONSISTENT communication over time. A few texts from last week won't cut it. Aim to show communication spanning your entire relationship.

Photos Together

  • Photos together in person (minimum 15-20, ideally more)
Include: Different locations, different dates, with friends/family, casual + formal. Avoid: 50 selfies from the same day.

Relationship Timeline

  • Personal statement / cover letter (how you met, relationship development, engagement, future plans)
  • Engagement ring receipt / photos
  • Joint travel itineraries
  • Evidence of meeting each other's families
  • Wedding planning evidence
  • Affidavits from friends/family attesting to relationship

Part 4: I-129F Form Requirements

  • Form I-129F (completed, all sections)
  • Form G-325A (Biographic Information) for BOTH petitioner and beneficiary
  • Filing fee: $675 (check or money order to "U.S. Department of Homeland Security")
  • 1 passport photo of petitioner
  • 1 passport photo of beneficiary

Part 5: Consular Interview Documents

Needed AFTER I-129F approval, for the embassy interview.

  • DS-160 confirmation page
  • Visa appointment confirmation letter
  • Medical examination results (from authorized panel physician)
  • I-134 Affidavit of Support with: tax returns (3 years), W-2s, pay stubs, bank statements (3 months), employment letter
  • Police clearance certificate(s)
  • Original birth certificate
  • Valid passport
  • ALL relationship evidence (bring originals + organized copies)
  • Passport photos (2)
  • Visa fee receipt ($265)
Interview day: Arrive 30 min early. Dress business casual. Bring everything in a labeled folder. Answer directly — don't volunteer extra info. Both partners should know key dates (first meeting, engagement, birthdays).

Part 6: Post-Arrival & Adjustment of Status

After K-1 entry and marriage within 90 days.

  • Marriage certificate (certified copy)
  • Form I-485 (Adjustment of Status)
  • Form I-765 (Employment Authorization / EAD)
  • Form I-131 (Advance Parole / travel document)
  • Form I-864 (Affidavit of Support — more detailed than I-134)
  • Medical examination (if more than 1 year since original)
  • Passport photos (2)
  • Filing fee: $1,440 (covers I-485, I-765, I-131)
  • Copy of K-1 visa and I-94 arrival record
  • Additional relationship evidence (wedding photos, joint lease, joint bank account)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Not enough relationship evidence — #1 reason for RFEs. More is better.
  2. Expired passport — Must be valid 6+ months beyond planned entry.
  3. Missing prior marriage docs — EVERY prior marriage, BOTH partners.
  4. Wrong photo specs — Follow USCIS requirements exactly.
  5. Unsigned forms — Double-check every signature line.
  6. Wrong fee amount — Fees change. Verify at uscis.gov.
  7. Inconsistent information — Dates, addresses, names must match across all forms.
  8. Filing at wrong address — Check current USCIS lockbox address for your state.
