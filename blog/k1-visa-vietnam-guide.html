K-1 Visa from Vietnam: Complete 2026 Guide

Last updated: March 2026 · 14 min read

Quick Answer

The K-1 visa process from Vietnam takes 12-17 months and costs $2,500-$4,200 total. All interviews are at the U.S. Consulate in Ho Chi Minh City. Vietnamese applicants need a Judicial Record Certificate (Phieu Ly Lich Tu Phap) from the Ministry of Public Security, their Household Registration Book, and a medical exam. Passports must be valid for at least 8 months beyond visa issuance.

Vietnam is one of the top countries for K-1 fiance visa petitions filed with USCIS. The large Vietnamese-American community — over 2 million strong, concentrated in California, Texas, and the Washington D.C. area — maintains strong ties to Vietnam, and cross-border relationships are common.

This guide covers everything specific to the Vietnamese K-1 visa process in 2026 — from the Ho Chi Minh City consulate procedures and the Judicial Record Certificate to the Household Registration Book requirement and medical exams. If you haven't confirmed eligibility, start with our K-1 visa requirements guide.

Why Vietnam Is Common for K-1 Visas

  • Large diaspora. The Vietnamese-American community is one of the largest Asian-American populations in the U.S., creating frequent cross-border relationships.
  • HCMC-only processing. All K-1 interviews happen at the U.S. Consulate in Ho Chi Minh City, even for applicants from Hanoi, Da Nang, or other northern and central regions.
  • Unique document requirements. Vietnam has its own system of civil documents including the Household Registration Book (So Ho Khau) and Judicial Record Certificate that are specific to the Vietnamese bureaucratic system.
  • No attorney at interview. The consulate explicitly prohibits legal representatives from attending the immigrant visa interview.
  • Packet 3 system. The HCMC consulate uses a specific instruction packet system (PKT3-K) that must be received before the applicant can proceed.

U.S. Consulate in Ho Chi Minh City

All K-1 visa interviews in Vietnam are conducted at the U.S. Consulate General in Ho Chi Minh City. The consulate's Immigrant Visa Unit handles all K-1 processing for the entire country. Contact: (028) 3520 4200.

The Vietnam-Specific Process

  1. Case arrives from NVC. After USCIS approval and NVC transfer, the HCMC consulate receives the file.
  2. Instruction Packet (PKT3-K). The consulate sends a detailed instruction packet with specific steps. Do not schedule an appointment until after receiving this packet.
  3. Complete DS-160. Fill out the DS-160 visa application online and pay the $265 fee.
  4. Gather Vietnamese documents. Collect all required documents including the Judicial Record Certificate, Household Registration Book, and birth certificate.
  5. Medical exam. Complete the medical exam at an authorized panel physician clinic.
  6. Interview. Attend the interview at the consulate with all original documents and copies.

Judicial Record Certificate (Phieu Ly Lich Tu Phap)

The Vietnamese police clearance is called the Judicial Record Certificate (Phieu Ly Lich Tu Phap). This is the Vietnamese equivalent of a police background check.

Important 2025 Change

As of March 1, 2025, the authority to issue Judicial Record Certificates was transferred from the Ministry of Justice to the Ministry of Public Security. Applications previously submitted through the Department of Justice or National Center for Judicial Records now go through the police system.

How to Get It

  1. Apply at the local police department where you are registered (based on your Household Registration Book).
  2. Bring required documents: National ID card (CCCD), Household Registration Book, and application form.
  3. Pay the fee: Approximately VND 200,000 (~$8).
  4. Processing time: 10-15 business days.
  5. For Ho Chi Minh City residents: Apply at the HCM City police office or Department of Public Security.

Important notes:

  • If you have lived outside Vietnam for 6+ months since age 16, you also need a police certificate from that country.
  • The certificate should be obtained within a few months of your interview date to ensure it is current.
  • The certificate must be accompanied by a certified English translation.

Medical Examination

The medical exam must be performed by a USCIS-designated panel physician in Vietnam. The consulate will provide specific clinic information in the instruction packet.

What is included:

  • Complete physical examination
  • Chest X-ray (tuberculosis screening — required for all applicants age 2+)
  • Blood tests (syphilis, HIV) for applicants 15+
  • Urine tests for applicants 15+
  • Vaccination history review

Required Vaccinations

  • MMR (measles, mumps, rubella)
  • Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis)
  • Varicella (chickenpox)
  • Influenza (seasonal)
  • COVID-19
  • Hepatitis A and B

Cost ranges from $150-$300 depending on required vaccinations. Bring any existing vaccination records to reduce costs. Results come in a sealed envelope — do not open it.

Interview Tips for Ho Chi Minh City

What to Expect

  1. Security screening. You will go through security upon arrival at the consulate.
  2. Fingerprinting. Your fingerprints will be taken at the consulate.
  3. Document submission. Submit all required documents and answer preliminary questions.
  4. Fee payment (if not already paid).
  5. Interview with an American officer. Questions about your relationship, background, and plans.

Vietnam-Specific Tips

  • No legal representatives allowed. The consulate explicitly prohibits attorneys or representatives from attending the interview. Only people listed on the appointment letter may enter.
  • Name your fiance on your photos. Write your name and date of birth on the back of each passport photo as instructed.
  • Bring extensive relationship evidence. Printed photos (with dates), communication logs, travel records, and evidence of visits to Vietnam or your partner's visits elsewhere.
  • Age gap scrutiny. If there is a significant age difference, be prepared with strong evidence of a genuine emotional relationship beyond financial support.
  • Know your petitioner's details. Full address, employer, salary, daily routine, family members.
  • Be honest about how you met. If you met through a matchmaking service or online, be straightforward about it. Consular officers deal with this regularly and appreciate honesty.

Common Issues and Denial Reasons

  • Relationship authenticity concerns. The HCMC consulate processes high volumes of K-1 cases and officers are experienced at identifying problematic cases. Large age gaps, brief relationships, or inability to communicate directly can raise flags.
  • Language barrier. If you and your fiance do not share a common language, this will be a point of questioning. Be prepared to explain how you communicate.
  • Prior immigration violations. Any previous overstays or immigration issues in any country must be disclosed.
  • Incomplete documentation. Missing the Household Registration Book or not having a recent enough Judicial Record Certificate can cause delays.
  • Financial insufficiency. The U.S. petitioner must meet income requirements on the I-134. See our I-134 guide.

After the Visa: What Happens Next

  1. Travel to the U.S. within 6 months of visa issuance.
  2. Marry within 90 days. See our 90-day rule guide.
  3. File Adjustment of Status (I-485, $1,440) after marriage.
  4. Apply for work authorization (EAD) concurrently.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the K-1 visa process take from Vietnam?

Approximately 14-18 months total: 8-10 months USCIS, 4-6 weeks NVC, and 2-4 months for the HCMC consulate phase.

Where is the interview conducted?

All K-1 interviews are at the U.S. Consulate General in Ho Chi Minh City. Applicants from Hanoi and elsewhere must travel to HCMC.

What police certificate do I need?

A Judicial Record Certificate (Phieu Ly Lich Tu Phap) from the Ministry of Public Security (changed from Ministry of Justice as of March 2025).

What is the Household Registration Book?

The So Ho Khau is a Vietnamese government document that records all members of a household at a registered address. You must bring the original and a copy to your interview.

Does my passport need to be valid for 8 months?

Yes. The HCMC consulate requires passports valid for at least 8 months beyond the visa issuance date, longer than the standard 6-month requirement at some other posts.

Can I bring a lawyer to the interview?

No. The HCMC consulate does not permit legal representatives to attend the immigrant visa interview. Only individuals listed on the appointment letter may enter.
