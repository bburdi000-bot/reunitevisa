K-1 Visa from South Korea: Complete 2026 Guide

Last updated: March 2026·13 min read

Quick Answer

The K-1 visa process from South Korea takes 12-17 months and costs $3,200-$4,800 total. Interviews are at the U.S. Embassy in Seoul. Korean applicants need a Criminal Investigation Records Check from the police (allow 2+ months processing), a medical exam, and online pre-registration before the interview. All Korean documents need certified English translations.

South Korea is a significant source of K-1 fiance visa petitions, driven by the long-standing U.S. military presence, growing cultural exchange, and strong Korean-American communities in cities like Los Angeles, New York, and the Washington D.C. area. The Seoul embassy processes K-1 cases efficiently, but the police certificate processing time requires early planning.

This guide covers the South Korea-specific K-1 process in 2026. If you haven't confirmed eligibility, start with our K-1 visa requirements guide.

Why South Korea Is Common for K-1 Visas

  • U.S. military presence. Over 28,000 U.S. troops are stationed in South Korea, creating many cross-cultural relationships.
  • Korean-American community. Over 1.8 million Korean-Americans maintain cultural connections with Korea.
  • Long police certificate processing. The Korean Criminal Investigation Records Check can take 2+ months — the longest of any major K-1 country. Plan accordingly.
  • Online pre-registration required. You must register online before the interview appointment to ensure correct passport and document return information.
  • One Cellphone Only policy. The Seoul embassy restricts what you can bring inside.

U.S. Embassy in Seoul

All K-1 interviews are at the U.S. Embassy in Seoul. The embassy schedules K-1 interviews through the NVC or via online scheduling for cases not scheduled by NVC.

Process

  1. Case arrives from NVC and interview is scheduled.
  2. Register online before your appointment (required for passport/document return).
  3. Schedule medical exam with an embassy-designated physician.
  4. Gather documents including the Criminal Investigation Records Check.
  5. Interview at the embassy.

Criminal Investigation Records Check

Korean nationals need the Criminal (Investigation) Records Check Reply "for permission of foreign country immigration and stay" (Bomjoi-Soosakyongryeok Hoiboseo: Waekuk Yipkuk-Chaeryu Heogayong).

How to Get It

  1. Visit any Korean police station.
  2. Request the specific type for foreign immigration purposes (not the standard domestic version).
  3. Bring your passport and one photo.
  4. Processing time: 2+ months. This is significantly longer than most countries. Apply as early as possible.
  5. Pick up at the same police station.
The 2+ month processing time for the Korean police certificate is the biggest planning challenge. Apply as soon as your I-129F is approved, even before you receive the embassy instruction packet. Do not wait.

Medical Examination

Schedule the medical exam with an embassy-designated panel physician as soon as you have an appointment date. The panel physician will provide results in a sealed envelope — do NOT open it.

Cost: approximately $200-$400 (KRW 270,000-540,000) depending on vaccinations needed. Bring vaccination records to reduce costs.

Interview Tips for Seoul

  • One Cellphone Only policy. Check the embassy website for allowed/prohibited items before your visit.
  • Register online before the interview. This is mandatory for correct passport and document return.
  • All fiancee/K1/K2 applicants must attend the interview regardless of age.
  • Bring extensive relationship evidence. Photos, communication records, travel documentation.
  • Military connections: If the petitioner is U.S. military in Korea, bring military documentation.
  • Know your petitioner's details. Address, employer, salary, family members.

Common Issues

  • Police certificate delays. The 2+ month processing catches many applicants off guard. Apply early.
  • Wrong type of police certificate. You need the specific version for foreign immigration, not the standard domestic check.
  • Relationship evidence. Bring strong proof of a genuine relationship.
  • Financial insufficiency. See our I-134 guide.

After the Visa

  1. Travel to the U.S. within 6 months.
  2. Marry within 90 days. See our 90-day rule guide.
  3. File Adjustment of Status (I-485, $1,440).
  4. Apply for work authorization (EAD).

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take from South Korea?

Approximately 14-18 months, partly due to the 2+ month police certificate processing.

Where is the interview?

U.S. Embassy in Seoul.

Why does the police certificate take so long?

The Korean Criminal Investigation Records Check for immigration purposes requires thorough processing. Apply as early as possible — ideally as soon as USCIS approves your I-129F.

Do Korean documents need translation?

Yes. All Korean documents require certified English translations.

Can I bring my phone to the embassy?

One cellphone only. No tablets, laptops, or other electronics. Check the embassy website for full security rules.

What if the petitioner is U.S. military in Korea?

Military petitioners can file from Korea. Bring military ID, orders, and documentation of your relationship.
