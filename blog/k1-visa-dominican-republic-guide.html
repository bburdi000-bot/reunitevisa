The Dominican Republic is one of the top countries of origin for K-1 fiance visa petitions filed with USCIS. The close geographic proximity to the United States, large Dominican diaspora communities in cities like New York, Boston, and Miami, and strong cultural connections between the two countries mean thousands of Dominican-American couples navigate this process every year.

This guide covers everything specific to the Dominican Republic K-1 visa process in 2026 — from the Santo Domingo embassy procedures and police certificates to medical exams and the recent changes to Dominican civil documents. If you haven't confirmed your eligibility yet, start with our K-1 visa requirements guide first.

Why the Dominican Republic Is Common for K-1 Visas

Several factors make the Dominican Republic a high-volume K-1 visa country:

  • Large diaspora. Over 2 million people of Dominican descent live in the United States, creating frequent cross-border relationships and family connections.
  • Geographic proximity. A 3-4 hour flight from the East Coast makes in-person visits easier to maintain, which strengthens K-1 petitions with evidence of a genuine relationship.
  • High scrutiny for fraud. Because of the volume of petitions, the Santo Domingo embassy has experienced officers who look closely at relationship authenticity. This is good for legitimate couples — it means your case will be handled by professionals who know the process well.
  • Spanish-language documents. All Dominican civil documents are in Spanish and require certified English translations, adding a step (and cost) compared to English-speaking countries.
  • 2025 civil document changes. The Dominican Republic introduced a new unified civil document format effective July 1, 2025, which affects how documents are prepared and submitted.

U.S. Embassy in Santo Domingo

All K-1 visa interviews in the Dominican Republic are conducted at the U.S. Embassy in Santo Domingo, located at Av. Republica de Colombia #57, Santo Domingo. There is no other consular post in the Dominican Republic that processes immigrant or fiance visas.

The Process at Santo Domingo

  1. Case arrives from NVC. After USCIS approves the I-129F and the NVC forwards the case, the Santo Domingo embassy receives the file and sends your fiance an instruction packet with specific steps.
  2. Complete DS-160. Your fiance fills out the DS-160 visa application online and pays the $265 visa fee.
  3. Gather documents. Collect all required Dominican documents (detailed below), get translations, and obtain the police certificate.
  4. Medical exam. Complete the medical examination with an embassy-authorized panel physician.
  5. Interview. Attend the visa interview at the embassy. In-person interviews are required for all K-1 applicants as of October 2025.

Police Certificate: Certificado de Buena Conducta

The Dominican police certificate is officially called the Certificado de Buena Conducta (Certificate of Good Conduct), issued by the Procuraduria General de la Republica (Attorney General's Office). This document confirms the applicant has no criminal record in the Dominican Republic.

How to Get It

  1. Pay the fee. Pay online through the Attorney General's website or at a branch of Banco de Reservas de la Republica Dominicana. The fee is approximately RD$500-1,000 (~$9-$17).
  2. Visit a CAC office. Go to any Centro de Atencion al Ciudadano (CAC) of the Procuraduria General. Bring your Dominican national identification card (cedula), a photocopy of both sides, and your payment receipt.
  3. Receive the certificate. Processing typically takes 3-5 business days, though it can sometimes be same-day at less busy locations.

Important notes:

  • The certificate must be issued less than 2 years before your interview date.
  • If you are 16 years or older, this document is required.
  • If you have lived in any other country for 6+ months since age 16, you will also need a police certificate from that country.
  • The certificate must be accompanied by a certified English translation.

Medical Examination

Your fiance must complete a medical examination with a USCIS-designated panel physician in the Dominican Republic before the interview. The embassy will provide a list of authorized physicians in the instruction packet.

What is included in the exam:

  • Complete physical examination
  • Chest X-ray (tuberculosis screening)
  • Blood tests (syphilis, HIV, and others as indicated)
  • Vaccination history review
  • Mental health screening

Required Vaccinations

The panel physician will verify vaccination records and administer any missing required vaccines:

  • MMR (measles, mumps, rubella)
  • Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis)
  • Varicella (chickenpox)
  • Influenza (seasonal)
  • COVID-19
  • Hepatitis A and B
  • Polio (if childhood series incomplete)

Scheduling and Tips

  • Schedule the medical exam 2-3 weeks before your interview date as recommended by the embassy.
  • Bring your passport, two passport-style photos, the embassy instruction letter, vaccination records (if available), and payment.
  • Cost ranges from $200-$350 depending on which vaccinations you need.
  • Results are provided in a sealed envelope. Do not open it. Bring it sealed to your interview.

Interview Tips for Santo Domingo

What to Expect on Interview Day

  1. Arrive early. Plan to arrive 30 minutes before your scheduled appointment. Lines can be long, especially on busy days.
  2. Security screening. No electronics (phones, smartwatches) are allowed inside. Leave them at home or with someone outside.
  3. Document submission. You will submit your documents at a window, then wait to be called for the interview.
  4. The interview. A consular officer will ask questions about your relationship, plans, and background. See our complete guide to K-1 interview questions.

Santo Domingo-Specific Tips

  • Fraud awareness is high. The Santo Domingo embassy processes a large volume of K-1 cases and officers are experienced at identifying inauthentic relationships. Be genuine and specific about your relationship.
  • Show the emotional connection. Officers want to see a real relationship, not a transactional arrangement. Share specific stories, details about your partner's daily life, and authentic reasons for your relationship.
  • Bring physical evidence. Printed photos with dates and locations, greeting cards, ticket stubs from trips together, and any other tangible relationship proof.
  • Know your petitioner's details. Full address, employer, salary, birthday, family members. These are standard verification questions.
  • Speak confidently in your language. Interviews can be conducted in Spanish with an interpreter available if needed, but answer clearly and directly regardless of language.
  • If you have a prior visa denial, prepare an explanation. A previous tourist visa denial is not disqualifying for a K-1, but be ready to explain it honestly.

Common Issues and Denial Reasons

The most common issues for Dominican K-1 applicants include:

  • Insufficient evidence of genuine relationship. This is the number one reason for denials worldwide, but the Santo Domingo embassy is particularly thorough. Bring extensive evidence: photos, communication logs, travel records, and testimony from people who know you as a couple.
  • Document format issues. With the 2025 civil document changes, ensure your documents are in the correct format (Acta Inextensa for pre-July 2025 documents, new unified format for later documents).
  • Translation errors. Poor or inaccurate translations can cause delays or requests for additional evidence. Use a qualified, certified translator.
  • Incomplete vaccination records. If you cannot locate childhood vaccination records, the panel physician will need to administer multiple vaccines, which increases cost and can delay the process if appointments are backlogged.
  • Prior immigration violations. Any previous overstays, unauthorized work, or immigration violations in the U.S. or elsewhere must be disclosed and can complicate your case.
  • Financial insufficiency. The U.S. petitioner must demonstrate income above the poverty guidelines on the I-134 Affidavit of Support. See our I-134 guide for details.

After the Visa: What Happens Next

Once your Dominican fiance's K-1 visa is approved and stamped in their passport:

  1. Travel to the U.S. within 6 months. The K-1 visa is valid for a single entry within 6 months of issuance.
  2. Marry within 90 days. You must legally marry within 90 days of your fiance's arrival in the United States. This can be a courthouse ceremony or a full wedding — as long as it is a legally valid marriage.
  3. File Adjustment of Status. After marriage, file I-485 (Adjustment of Status) with the $1,440 filing fee to begin the green card process. Learn about the full 90-day rule and what happens after arrival.
  4. Apply for work authorization. You can file for an Employment Authorization Document (EAD) concurrently with the I-485.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the K-1 visa process take from the Dominican Republic?

The total process takes approximately 13-17 months: 8-10 months for USCIS processing, 4-6 weeks for NVC transfer, and 2-3 months for the Santo Domingo embassy phase. After visa issuance, your fiance has 6 months to enter the U.S.

How much does a K-1 visa cost from the Dominican Republic?

Total cost is approximately $2,890-$3,460 without professional help. This includes all government fees, medical exam, Dominican documents, translations, and a one-way flight. See the detailed cost table above.

Where is the K-1 visa interview conducted?

All K-1 visa interviews are conducted at the U.S. Embassy in Santo Domingo at Av. Republica de Colombia #57.

What police certificate do I need?

You need a Certificado de Buena Conducta from the Procuraduria General de la Republica. Apply at any CAC office with your cedula and payment receipt. It must be less than 2 years old at the time of your interview.

Do Dominican documents need to be translated?

Yes. All documents in Spanish must have a certified English translation. The translator must include a statement certifying their competence and the translation's accuracy.

What changed with Dominican civil documents in 2025?

The Dominican Republic introduced a new unified civil document format effective July 1, 2025. Documents issued before that date must be in the Acta Inextensa format. Documents in the new format do not require separate legalization for visa purposes.

Can my fiance get a K-1 visa if they were previously married?

Yes. Unlike the Philippines, the Dominican Republic allows divorce. Your fiance must provide the original divorce decree and a certified English translation proving all previous marriages have been legally terminated.
