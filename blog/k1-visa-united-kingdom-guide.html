K-1 Visa from the United Kingdom: Complete 2026 Guide

Last updated: March 2026 · 13 min read

Quick Answer

The K-1 visa process from the UK takes 12-16 months and costs $3,000-$4,800 total. All interviews are at the U.S. Embassy in London at 33 Nine Elms Lane. UK applicants need an ACRO police certificate and a medical exam 2 weeks before the interview. A unique London requirement: you must attend a Document Review at the embassy on the same day as your medical exam. No translation costs since documents are in English.

The United Kingdom is a significant source of K-1 fiance visa petitions, driven by the strong cultural and language connections between the U.S. and UK. American-British couples benefit from several advantages in the K-1 process: documents are already in English, the London embassy is well-organized, and the shared language eliminates communication concerns that can complicate cases from other countries.

This guide covers everything specific to the UK K-1 visa process in 2026, including the London embassy's unique combined medical-and-document-review requirement, the ACRO police certificate, and the vaccination exemption for K-1 applicants. If you haven't confirmed eligibility, start with our K-1 visa requirements guide.

Why the UK Is Unique for K-1 Visas

  • English-language advantage. All UK documents are in English, eliminating translation costs and the risk of translation errors that affect applicants from non-English-speaking countries.
  • Combined medical and document review. London requires you to attend a Document Review at the embassy on the same day as your medical exam, 2 weeks before the actual interview. This is a unique two-visit process.
  • Vaccination exemption for K-1. K-1 applicants at the London embassy are NOT required to meet vaccination requirements at the interview stage. You will need them later when filing for Adjustment of Status in the U.S.
  • ACRO police certificate. The UK uses its own police certificate system through the ACRO Criminal Records Office rather than a standard police clearance.
  • Well-organized process. The London embassy has a structured, efficient K-1 process that tends to move smoothly for well-prepared applicants.

U.S. Embassy in London

All K-1 visa interviews in the United Kingdom are conducted at the U.S. Embassy in London, located at 33 Nine Elms Lane, London SW11 7US. Plan to arrive 30 minutes before your appointment for airport-style security. Expect to be at the embassy for approximately 2-3 hours.

The London Two-Visit Process

  1. Case arrives from NVC. The London embassy receives the file and sends instructions.
  2. Schedule medical exam. Call 020 7486 7822 (Mon-Fri, 9am-5pm) to schedule. Quote your case number (starts with LND).
  3. Visit 1: Medical exam + Document Review (2 weeks before interview). Complete the medical exam, then attend the Document Review at the Immigrant Visa Unit on the same day with all original documents and photocopies.
  4. Visit 2: Visa Interview. Return to the embassy for the actual interview, bringing all original documents.

ACRO Police Certificate

The ACRO (Association of Chief Police Officers Criminal Records Office) police certificate is the UK's police clearance document required for all K-1 applicants aged 16 and older.

How to Get It

  1. Apply online at acro.police.uk.
  2. Provide identification and personal details as requested.
  3. Pay the fee: 55 GBP (~$69).
  4. Processing time: Typically 10-15 business days, though it can take up to 6 weeks.

Important notes:

  • The ACRO certificate must have been issued within the last 2 years (24 months).
  • You must bring it to both the medical exam/document review and the interview.
  • If you have lived in other countries for 6+ months since age 16, you need police certificates from those countries too.
  • Apply early — processing times can be unpredictable.

Medical Examination

The medical exam must be completed at least 2 weeks before your interview date. Only a physician approved by the U.S. Embassy can perform the exam. Schedule by calling 020 7486 7822 and quoting your case number.

What to bring to the medical exam:

  • Photo identification (passport or driving licence)
  • Four photographs meeting Department of State requirements
  • ACRO police certificate (issued within past 24 months)
  • Vaccination records from your GP

Vaccination Exemption for K-1 Applicants

This is a key advantage of the London process: K-1 visa applicants are NOT required to meet vaccination requirements at the time of the visa interview. You will, however, need to meet vaccination requirements when you file for Adjustment of Status with USCIS after arriving in the United States. This means you can complete the vaccinations at a U.S. doctor's office, which may be more convenient and potentially covered by insurance after marriage.

The medical exam itself costs approximately 300-500 GBP ($375-$625) depending on any additional tests required. Since vaccinations are not mandatory at this stage, costs tend to be lower than at many other posts.

Interview Tips for London

  • Arrive 30 minutes early. The embassy conducts airport-style security screening.
  • Bring all originals. You must bring all original documents to both the Document Review (Visit 1) and the Interview (Visit 2).
  • The English advantage. Since you and the officer share a common language, the interview tends to be more conversational. Use this to your advantage by being personable and detailed about your relationship.
  • Relationship evidence. Even though UK cases may seem "easier" due to language, you still need strong evidence: photos, travel records, communication logs, and testimony from people who know you as a couple.
  • Know your petitioner's details. Address, employer, salary, daily routine, family members. Standard questions apply regardless of country.
  • Prior visa history. If you have traveled to the U.S. before on ESTA/B-1/B-2, be prepared to discuss previous visits and how your relationship developed.

Common Issues and Denial Reasons

  • Complacency. Because the UK process feels "easier" than many countries, some couples under-prepare. Treat it with the same seriousness as any K-1 case.
  • Insufficient relationship evidence. Having a shared language does not replace documented proof of a genuine relationship.
  • Missing the Document Review. Forgetting that London requires a separate Document Review visit (on the same day as the medical exam, 2 weeks before the interview) is a common logistical error.
  • Expired ACRO certificate. The 24-month validity seems generous but plan accordingly.
  • Financial insufficiency. See our I-134 guide.

After the Visa: What Happens Next

  1. Travel to the U.S. within 6 months.
  2. Marry within 90 days. See our 90-day rule guide.
  3. Complete vaccinations. Since K-1 applicants from London are exempt at the interview stage, you must meet vaccination requirements when filing I-485.
  4. File Adjustment of Status (I-485, $1,440) after marriage.
  5. Apply for work authorization (EAD) concurrently.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the K-1 process take from the UK?

Approximately 13-16 months: 8-10 months USCIS, 4-6 weeks NVC, and 2-3 months for the London embassy phase.

Where is the interview?

U.S. Embassy London, 33 Nine Elms Lane, London SW11 7US.

What is the ACRO certificate?

The UK police certificate, issued by the ACRO Criminal Records Office. Apply online at acro.police.uk. Costs 55 GBP and must be issued within the last 2 years.

Do I need vaccinations for the London interview?

No. K-1 applicants at London are exempt from vaccination requirements at the interview stage. You will need to meet them when filing for Adjustment of Status in the U.S.

What is the Document Review?

A unique London requirement: on the same day as your medical exam (2 weeks before your interview), you must attend a Document Review at the embassy's Immigrant Visa Unit with all original documents and copies.

Do I need to translate my UK documents?

No. UK documents are in English and accepted as-is. This saves $50-$200 compared to non-English-speaking countries.
