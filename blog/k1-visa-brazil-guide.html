K-1 Visa from Brazil: Complete 2026 Guide

Last updated: March 2026 · 14 min read

Quick Answer

The K-1 visa process from Brazil takes 12-17 months and costs $2,800-$4,500 total. All interviews happen at the U.S. Consulate in Rio de Janeiro, regardless of where your fiance lives in Brazil. Applicants must complete biometrics at an ASC before the interview. Brazilian applicants need federal and state police certificates, and all Portuguese documents require certified English translations.

Brazil is one of the top countries of origin for K-1 fiance visa petitions. The large Brazilian-American community, particularly in Florida, Massachusetts, and the New York/New Jersey area, creates a steady flow of cross-border relationships. Thousands of Brazilian-American couples navigate the K-1 process every year.

This guide covers everything specific to the Brazilian K-1 visa process in 2026 — from the unique requirement to interview in Rio de Janeiro regardless of where your fiance lives, to the ASC biometrics appointment, police certificates, and Portuguese document translations. If you haven't confirmed eligibility yet, start with our K-1 visa requirements guide.

Why Brazil Is Common for K-1 Visas

  • Large diaspora. Over 1.7 million Brazilian-born immigrants live in the United States, with major communities in South Florida, the Boston metro area, and New Jersey.
  • Rio-only processing. All K-1 interviews happen in Rio de Janeiro, meaning applicants from Sao Paulo, Brasilia, Manaus, or anywhere else in Brazil must travel there — adding logistical complexity and cost.
  • ASC biometrics requirement. Brazil requires a pre-interview biometrics collection at an Application Support Center (ASC), which is an extra step not found in every country.
  • Portuguese documents. All civil documents are in Portuguese and require certified English translations.
  • Multiple police certificates. Brazilian applicants need both a federal police certificate and state-level certificates from each state where they have resided.

U.S. Consulate in Rio de Janeiro

All K-1 visa interviews in Brazil are conducted at the U.S. Consulate General in Rio de Janeiro, located at Av. Presidente Wilson, 147, Centro, Rio de Janeiro. This is the only post in Brazil that processes K-1 fiance visas, even though the U.S. maintains an embassy in Brasilia and consulates in Sao Paulo, Recife, and Porto Alegre.

The Brazil-Specific Process

  1. Case arrives from NVC. After USCIS approval and NVC transfer, the Rio consulate receives the file and sends your fiance instructions via email.
  2. Schedule ASC appointment. Your fiance must visit an Application Support Center to have their photo and fingerprints collected. This must be done before the interview can be scheduled.
  3. Schedule interview. After completing ASC biometrics, schedule the visa interview at the Rio consulate.
  4. Complete DS-160. Fill out the DS-160 online. Starting May 2025, the DS-160 confirmation number must match the one used to make the appointment.
  5. Medical exam. Schedule and complete the medical exam with an embassy-designated panel physician.
  6. Interview. Attend the interview at the Rio consulate with all original documents. As of July 2025, applicants who arrive without all required original documents will not be interviewed and must reschedule.
Critical 2025 change: Starting July 1, 2025, if you arrive at the consulate without ALL required original documents, you will not be interviewed and must schedule a new appointment. Prepare everything in advance.

Police Certificates: Federal and State

Brazil requires two types of police certificates for K-1 visa applicants:

Federal Police Certificate

  1. Go online to the Policia Federal website at gov.br/pf/pt-br/assuntos/antecedentes-criminais.
  2. Enter your CPF number or foreigner registration number (CIE).
  3. Download the certificate. It is issued immediately and free of charge.
  4. Important: The certificate is valid for only 90 days, so obtain it close to your interview date.

State Police Certificates

  • You need a state-level certificate from each Brazilian state where you have lived for more than six months during the last five years.
  • Apply at the Policia Civil office in each relevant state. Requirements and costs vary by state.
  • Processing time is typically 3-10 business days.
  • Some states offer online applications; others require in-person visits.

Medical Examination

Your fiance must complete a medical examination with a USCIS-designated panel physician in Brazil. The consulate will provide a list of authorized physicians and specific scheduling instructions.

What is included:

  • Complete physical examination
  • Chest X-ray (tuberculosis screening)
  • Blood tests (syphilis, HIV, and others)
  • Vaccination history review and administration of missing vaccines
  • Mental health screening

Required Vaccinations

  • MMR (measles, mumps, rubella)
  • Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis)
  • Varicella (chickenpox)
  • Influenza (seasonal)
  • COVID-19
  • Hepatitis A and B
  • Yellow fever (commonly already administered in Brazil)

Cost ranges from $200-$400 depending on which vaccinations are needed. Brazilian applicants who received childhood vaccines through the SUS (public health system) should bring their vaccination booklet (caderneta de vacinacao) to potentially reduce costs.

Interview Tips for Rio de Janeiro

What to Expect

  1. Arrive with all originals. As of July 2025, the consulate will not interview applicants who arrive without all required original documents.
  2. Security screening. Plan for airport-style security. Leave unnecessary electronics and large bags at your hotel.
  3. Document review first. Your documents will be reviewed before you are called for the actual interview.
  4. The interview itself. A consular officer will ask about your relationship, how you met, your plans, and your background. See our interview questions guide.

Brazil-Specific Tips

  • Plan your Rio trip carefully. If your fiance does not live in Rio, budget for flights, hotel (2-3 nights recommended), and local transportation. The ASC appointment and interview may be on different days.
  • DS-160 accuracy is critical. The confirmation number on your DS-160 must match the one used to schedule the appointment. Double-check this before interview day.
  • Bring extensive relationship evidence. Printed photos with dates, communication logs, travel records, receipts from trips together, and letters from family and friends who know your relationship.
  • Be prepared in English or Portuguese. Interviews can be conducted in either language. Answer clearly and directly.
  • Know your petitioner's details. Full address, employer, salary, daily routine, family members' names and ages.

Common Issues and Denial Reasons

  • Missing documents at interview. The new July 2025 rule means you will be turned away without all originals. Prepare a complete checklist and verify everything the night before.
  • Expired federal police certificate. The 90-day validity catches many applicants off guard. Time it carefully.
  • Missing state police certificates. You need one from every state where you lived 6+ months in the past 5 years. Do not forget states where you attended university or worked temporarily.
  • Translation quality. Only use a tradutor juramentado (sworn translator) for official documents. Informal translations will be rejected.
  • Insufficient relationship evidence. As with all K-1 cases, the core question is whether your relationship is genuine. Bring more evidence than you think you need.
  • Financial insufficiency. The U.S. petitioner must meet the income requirements on the I-134. See our I-134 guide.

After the Visa: What Happens Next

  1. Travel to the U.S. within 6 months of visa issuance.
  2. Marry within 90 days of arrival. See our 90-day rule guide.
  3. File Adjustment of Status (I-485, $1,440 fee) after marriage.
  4. Apply for work authorization (EAD) concurrently with the I-485.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the K-1 visa process take from Brazil?

Approximately 14-18 months total: 8-10 months USCIS processing, 4-6 weeks NVC transfer, and 2-4 months for the Rio consulate phase including ASC biometrics.

Where is the K-1 visa interview conducted in Brazil?

All K-1 interviews are at the U.S. Consulate General in Rio de Janeiro at Av. Presidente Wilson, 147, Centro. No other post in Brazil processes K-1 visas.

What is the ASC biometrics requirement?

Before the interview, your fiance must visit an Application Support Center (ASC) to have their photo and fingerprints collected. This step must be completed before the interview can be scheduled.

What police certificates do I need from Brazil?

You need the federal police certificate (free, from the Policia Federal website, valid 90 days) plus state-level certificates from each state where you lived 6+ months in the past 5 years.

Do Brazilian documents need translation?

Yes. All Portuguese documents must have certified English translations by a tradutor juramentado (sworn translator).

Can my fiance get a K-1 if they were previously married?

Yes. Brazil allows divorce. Provide the original divorce decree and a certified English translation.
