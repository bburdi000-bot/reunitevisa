The Philippines is consistently one of the top countries for K-1 fiance visa petitions. Thousands of Filipino-American couples navigate this process every year, and the U.S. Embassy in Manila processes more K-1 visas than almost any other post in the world.

That familiarity is both good and bad. Good because the Manila embassy has a well-oiled machine for processing these cases. Bad because the officers have seen every type of fraud and know exactly what to look for.

This guide covers everything specific to the Philippine K-1 visa process in 2026 — from NBI clearances and CENOMAR certificates to the Manila embassy interview and the unique challenge of Philippine annulment law. If you haven't yet confirmed your eligibility, start with our K-1 visa requirements guide first.

Why the Philippines Is Unique for K-1 Visas

Several factors make the Philippine K-1 process different from other countries:

  • No divorce law. The Philippines is one of only two countries in the world (along with Vatican City) that does not permit divorce. If your Filipino fiance was previously married, they must obtain a court annulment — a process that can take years and cost thousands of dollars.
  • High volume of petitions. The Manila embassy processes a very high number of K-1 cases, which means officers are experienced but also vigilant about fraud.
  • CFO (Commission on Filipinos Overseas) requirement. Filipino fiances approved for a K-1 visa must attend a CFO guidance and counseling session before departing the Philippines.
  • Specific document requirements. The Philippines has its own versions of standard documents: NBI clearance (instead of a generic "police clearance"), CENOMAR (certificate of no marriage), and PSA-issued civil documents.

Step-by-Step Process

Here is the complete K-1 visa process for a Filipino fiance, from start to finish:

Step 1: File I-129F with USCIS (U.S. Citizen)

The U.S. citizen petitioner files Form I-129F, Petition for Alien Fiance(e), with USCIS. This is filed from the United States with the $675 filing fee. Include evidence of your relationship, proof of citizenship, proof you've met in person, and evidence that both parties are free to marry.

Processing time: 8-10 months at the California or Texas Service Center (as of early 2026). Check our processing time tracker for the latest numbers.

Step 2: USCIS Approval → NVC Transfer

Once USCIS approves the I-129F, it is forwarded to the National Visa Center (NVC). The NVC assigns a case number and forwards the file to the U.S. Embassy in Manila. This transfer takes 4-6 weeks.

Step 3: Embassy Receives Case → Instruction Packet

The Manila embassy sends your fiance (the beneficiary) an instruction packet (sometimes called "Packet 3" or appointment instructions) with details on completing the DS-160, scheduling the medical exam, and gathering required documents.

Step 4: Complete DS-160 and Pay Visa Fee

Your fiance completes the DS-160 visa application online and pays the $265 visa application fee. The DS-160 must be completed carefully — errors or inconsistencies between the DS-160 and the I-129F are a common cause of interview problems.

Step 5: Gather Philippine Documents

This is where the Philippines-specific requirements come in. Your fiance needs to collect the NBI clearance, CENOMAR, PSA birth certificate, and other documents (detailed below).

Step 6: Medical Examination

Your fiance completes the medical exam at an authorized panel physician clinic in Manila (detailed below). This must be done before the interview.

Step 7: Interview at U.S. Embassy Manila

Your fiance attends the visa interview at the U.S. Embassy in Manila. If approved, the visa is stamped in their passport.

Step 8: CFO Guidance and Counseling

Before departing, your fiance must register with and attend a guidance and counseling session at the Commission on Filipinos Overseas (CFO). This is mandatory for all Filipino fiances and spouses of foreign nationals.

Step 9: Travel to the United States

Your fiance has 6 months from visa issuance to enter the United States. Upon entry, you have 90 days to marry.

Step 10: Marry and File Adjustment of Status

Get married within 90 days, then file I-485 (Adjustment of Status) with the $1,440 filing fee to begin the green card process. Learn about all the K-1 visa costs involved.

NBI Clearance: How to Get It

The National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) clearance is the Philippine equivalent of a police background check. Here is how to obtain it:

  1. Register online at the NBI Clearance website (https://clearance.nbi.gov.ph). Create an account and fill in your personal information.
  2. Select an NBI branch and appointment date. Choose a branch convenient to you. Manila, Makati, and Cebu branches tend to be the busiest.
  3. Pay the fee online. The fee is PHP 160 (~$3). Payment can be made through GCash, Maya, or over-the-counter at 7-Eleven, Bayad Center, or other payment partners.
  4. Visit the NBI branch on your appointment date. Bring a valid government ID and the payment receipt. You will have your photo and fingerprints taken.
  5. Receive your clearance. If there are no "hits" (matching records in the database), your clearance will be available for pickup the same day or within 1-2 days. If there is a hit (common with popular names), you'll need to return in 7-10 business days after the record is manually verified.

Important tips:

  • NBI clearances are valid for 1 year from the date of issue. Time it so it will still be valid on your interview date.
  • If you have lived outside the Philippines for 6+ months since age 16, you will also need a police clearance from that country.
  • NBI clearances for visa purposes should be requested specifically for "travel/visa" purposes.

CENOMAR: Certificate of No Marriage

The CENOMAR (Certificate of No Marriage Record, officially called "Advisory on Marriages") is a document issued by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) certifying that no marriage record exists for the person in the PSA's civil registry database.

Why it matters: This document proves your fiance is legally single and eligible to marry. It is required by the U.S. Embassy for the K-1 visa interview.

How to Get a CENOMAR

  1. Online (recommended): Go to PSA Serbilis (https://www.psaserbilis.com.ph), create an account, and request a CENOMAR. Pay PHP 210 (~$4) plus delivery fee. It will be delivered to your Philippine address within 5-7 business days.
  2. In person: Visit any PSA Serbilis outlet or SM Business Center location. Bring a valid ID. Same-day release is sometimes available but not guaranteed.

Important notes:

  • The CENOMAR only covers marriages registered with the PSA. A church wedding that was never civilly registered may not appear.
  • Make sure the CENOMAR is recent — ideally issued within 6 months of your interview date.
  • If a marriage record does appear on the CENOMAR, you will need to resolve it (through annulment or by proving it was registered in error) before proceeding.

What is included in the exam:

  • Complete physical examination
  • Chest X-ray (to screen for tuberculosis)
  • Blood tests (syphilis, HIV, and others as needed)
  • Review of vaccination history
  • Mental health screening

Required Vaccinations

The panel physician will check your vaccination records and administer any required vaccines you're missing. Common required vaccinations include:

  • MMR (measles, mumps, rubella)
  • Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis)
  • Varicella (chickenpox)
  • Influenza (seasonal)
  • COVID-19
  • Hepatitis A and B
  • Polio (if incomplete childhood series)

Additional vaccination costs can add PHP 2,000-15,000 ($35-$270) depending on which vaccines you need. If you have your childhood vaccination records (yellow card), bring them to potentially reduce costs.

How to Schedule

  1. Schedule your medical exam appointment after you receive your embassy instruction packet but before your interview date.
  2. Book at least 2-3 weeks before your interview to allow time for results and any follow-up tests.
  3. Bring: passport, 2 passport-style photos, embassy instruction letter, vaccination records (if available), and payment.

The clinic will provide your results in a sealed envelope. Do not open the envelope. Bring it sealed to your embassy interview.

The Manila Embassy Interview

All K-1 visa interviews in the Philippines take place at the U.S. Embassy in Manila, located at 1201 Roxas Boulevard, Ermita, Manila. There is no U.S. consulate in Cebu, Davao, or other cities that handles immigrant visa interviews.

What to Expect on Interview Day

  1. Arrive early. Lines at the Manila embassy can be long. Plan to arrive at least 30 minutes before your scheduled time.
  2. Security screening. No electronics (phones, smartwatches, tablets) are allowed inside the embassy. Leave them at home, in your car, or at nearby locker services. Bags must be small (no backpacks).
  3. Document submission. You'll submit your documents at one window, then wait to be called for the interview.
  4. The interview. A consular officer will ask questions about your relationship, your plans, and your background. See our complete guide to K-1 visa interview questions for detailed preparation.
  5. The decision. You will typically be told at the end of the interview whether you are approved, denied, or placed in administrative processing (needing additional documents).

Manila-Specific Interview Tips

  • Officers at Manila are experienced. They process thousands of K-1 cases per year. Don't try to be clever or use rehearsed scripts. They've heard it all.
  • Be genuine about your relationship. Manila officers are particularly attuned to transactional-sounding relationships. Emphasize emotional connection, shared experiences, and specific personal details about your partner.
  • The "why" matters more than the "what." Why do you love this person? Why are you willing to leave the Philippines? Authentic emotional answers carry more weight than lists of facts.
  • Bring physical evidence. Printed photos (labeled with dates and locations), greeting cards, letters, small gifts from your partner — tangible evidence of a real relationship.
  • Know your petitioner's details. Birthday, full address, employer, salary, family members' names, daily routine. These are standard verification questions.
  • If you have a previous tourist visa denial, address it. Many Filipino applicants have been denied B-1/B-2 tourist visas. This is not disqualifying for a K-1, but be ready to explain what changed (you now have a genuine fiance petitioning for you).

The Divorce Problem: Annulment in the Philippines

This is the biggest obstacle unique to Filipino applicants. The Philippines does not allow divorce (except for Muslim Filipinos under the Code of Muslim Personal Laws). If your Filipino fiance was previously married, their only option to become "legally free to marry" is:

Option 1: Court Annulment

  • What it is: A legal proceeding in a Philippine Regional Trial Court that declares the previous marriage void from the beginning.
  • Grounds: Psychological incapacity (the most commonly cited ground, under Article 36 of the Family Code), fraud, lack of parental consent (if married under 21), force or intimidation, impotence, or sexually transmitted disease.
  • Timeline: 1-3 years from filing to final court decision.
  • Cost: PHP 150,000-500,000 ($2,700-$9,000) including attorney fees, court fees, psychological evaluation, and other expenses.
  • After the annulment: The court decision must be registered with the Local Civil Registrar and the PSA. Only after PSA registration will the CENOMAR show "no marriage."

Option 2: Recognition of Foreign Divorce

  • What it is: If your Filipino fiance's former spouse is a foreign national, and the foreign spouse obtained a divorce in their country, the Filipino spouse can petition a Philippine court to recognize that foreign divorce.
  • Timeline: 6-18 months.
  • Cost: PHP 80,000-200,000 ($1,400-$3,600).
  • Limitation: This only works if the former spouse was a foreign national. If both parties were Filipino citizens, a foreign divorce is generally not recognized.
If your Filipino fiance needs an annulment, factor this into your timeline and budget early. It is the single most common reason Philippines K-1 cases are delayed by years. Contact us to discuss strategy.

Tips Specific to Filipino Applicants

Start Documents Early

Don't wait until USCIS approves the I-129F to start gathering Philippine documents. Order your PSA birth certificate, CENOMAR, and NBI clearance as soon as the petition is filed. If anything needs correction (wrong spelling, missing records), you'll have months to fix it.

Plan for Manila Travel

If your fiance lives outside Metro Manila (Visayas, Mindanao, or rural Luzon), budget for travel to Manila for the embassy interview and medical exam. Many applicants arrive 2-3 days early to handle everything without rushing. Budget for hotel, food, and local transportation.

Phone Lockers Near the Embassy

The U.S. Embassy does not allow electronics inside. Several businesses near the embassy at Roxas Boulevard offer locker services for PHP 50-100 where you can store your phone during the interview. Scope these out beforehand — don't leave your phone in a taxi.

CFO Counseling Is Mandatory

After visa approval, your fiance must register with the Commission on Filipinos Overseas (CFO) and attend a guidance and counseling program (GCP). This covers topics like adjusting to life in the U.S., your legal rights, and available support services. The CFO sticker in the passport is checked at Philippine airport departure — your fiance cannot leave the country without it.

Balikbayan Box

Many Filipino fiances ship personal belongings via balikbayan box services rather than paying for excess airline luggage. Companies like LBC, JRS Express, and ForEx Cargo offer door-to-door shipping from the Philippines to U.S. addresses. Budget PHP 3,000-5,000 ($55-$90) per box and allow 4-8 weeks for delivery.

Bring Enough Documents to the Interview

Manila embassy officers may ask for documents beyond the standard list. Bring extra relationship evidence: printed chat logs, additional photos, social media profiles showing your relationship, letters from family, and anything else that demonstrates a genuine relationship. It's better to have too much than too little.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the K-1 visa process take from the Philippines?

The total process takes approximately 14-18 months in 2026: 8-10 months for USCIS processing, 4-6 weeks for NVC transfer, and 2-4 months for the Manila embassy phase (documents, medical, interview). After visa issuance, your fiance has 6 months to enter the U.S.

How much does a K-1 visa cost from the Philippines?

Total cost is approximately $3,130-$4,330 without professional help. This includes all U.S. government fees ($2,380), medical exam ($150-$235 plus vaccinations), Philippine documents (~$50), and the one-way flight ($500-$1,200). See the detailed cost table above.

Where is the K-1 visa interview conducted in the Philippines?

All K-1 visa interviews are conducted at the U.S. Embassy in Manila at 1201 Roxas Boulevard, Ermita, Manila. No other location in the Philippines processes immigrant visas.

What is a CENOMAR and how do I get one?

CENOMAR (Certificate of No Marriage) is issued by the PSA and certifies no marriage record exists. Request it online at PSA Serbilis (https://www.psaserbilis.com.ph) for PHP 210 (~$4). Processing takes 5-7 business days.

Does the Philippines allow divorce?

No. Filipino citizens who were previously married must obtain a court annulment (1-3 years, PHP 150,000-500,000) or, if the former spouse is a foreign national, seek recognition of a foreign divorce (6-18 months, PHP 80,000-200,000).

What vaccinations are required for the medical exam?

Required vaccinations include MMR, Tdap, varicella, influenza, COVID-19, hepatitis A and B, and others based on age. Your panel physician at St. Luke's or another authorized clinic will review your records and administer any vaccines you're missing.
