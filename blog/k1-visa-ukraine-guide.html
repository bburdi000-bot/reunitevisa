K-1 Visa from Ukraine: Complete 2026 Guide

Last updated: March 2026 · 14 min read

Quick Answer

The K-1 visa process from Ukraine takes 13-18 months and costs $2,600-$4,300 total. The U.S. Embassy in Kyiv resumed K-1 processing in October 2024. Ukrainian applicants need a criminal record certificate from the Ministry of Internal Affairs and a medical exam from an embassy-approved physician. Martial law travel restrictions may affect male applicants aged 18-60.

Ukraine has historically been one of the top countries for K-1 fiance visa petitions. The ongoing conflict has added significant complexity to the process, but the U.S. Embassy in Kyiv resumed processing immigrant and fiance visa cases in late 2024, providing a path forward for Ukrainian-American couples.

This guide covers the Ukrainian K-1 visa process in 2026, including the impact of martial law on travel, the resumed Kyiv embassy operations, and the specific documents and procedures required. If you haven't confirmed eligibility, start with our K-1 visa requirements guide.

Ukraine K-1 Visas: Current Situation

  • Embassy operations resumed. As of October 2024, the U.S. Embassy in Kyiv resumed processing all immigrant and K visa cases for Ukrainian citizens and residents.
  • Reduced staffing. The embassy operates with reduced staffing, which may cause longer wait times for interview scheduling compared to pre-conflict processing.
  • Martial law restrictions. Ukraine's martial law includes travel restrictions that affect men aged 18-60, creating a unique challenge for male K-1 applicants.
  • Document accessibility. Some applicants from conflict-affected areas may face difficulty obtaining documents from local government offices. Alternative procedures may be available.
  • Safety considerations. Applicants must travel to Kyiv for the interview, and conditions may require security awareness and flexible scheduling.

U.S. Embassy in Kyiv

K-1 visa interviews are conducted at the U.S. Embassy in Kyiv. The embassy processes most immigrant visa cases for Ukrainian citizens, including K-1 and diversity visa cases.

The Process

  1. Case arrives from NVC. After USCIS approval and NVC transfer, the Kyiv embassy receives the file.
  2. K Visa Instructions. The embassy provides K Visa Instructions in English and Ukrainian with specific steps.
  3. Complete DS-160 and pay the $265 visa fee.
  4. Gather documents including criminal record certificate and all previous marriage termination documents.
  5. Medical exam with an embassy-accredited physician.
  6. Interview. Arrive no more than 15 minutes before your appointment time.

Martial Law and Travel Restrictions

This is the most significant unique consideration for Ukrainian K-1 applicants in 2026. Ukraine's martial law includes restrictions on men aged 18-60 leaving the country.

Key considerations:

  • Female applicants are generally not subject to the travel ban and can depart Ukraine after visa issuance.
  • Male applicants aged 18-60 face restrictions. There are certain exemption categories that have been modified multiple times since martial law was declared. Check the latest Ukrainian government regulations for current exemptions.
  • Visa issuance is separate from departure. The U.S. Embassy can issue a K-1 visa regardless of Ukrainian travel restrictions. However, your fiance must be able to actually leave Ukraine to use the visa.
  • Consult with both immigration and Ukrainian legal counsel if your male fiance is in the affected age range. This is a rapidly evolving area of policy.
The martial law travel restriction situation changes frequently. Verify the current rules with Ukrainian authorities before making plans. The K-1 visa itself remains valid for 6 months from issuance.

Criminal Record Certificate

Ukrainian applicants need a certificate confirming no criminal record, issued by the Ministry of Internal Affairs of Ukraine.

How to Get It

  1. Apply online or in person through the Ministry of Internal Affairs portal or at a local office.
  2. Provide identification: Internal passport, international passport, and application form.
  3. Processing time: Typically 10-30 business days, though wartime conditions may cause delays.
  4. Cost: Approximately UAH 200-400 (~$5-$10).

Notes:

  • If you have lived in other countries for 6+ months since age 16, you need police certificates from those countries too.
  • For applicants who have been displaced within Ukraine, alternative procedures may be available through relocated government offices.
  • The certificate should be obtained within a reasonable time before the interview.

Medical Examination

All K visa applicants must undergo a medical examination by a physician accredited by the U.S. Embassy in Kyiv. The embassy will provide the list of authorized physicians.

What is included:

  • Complete physical examination
  • Chest X-ray (tuberculosis screening)
  • Blood tests (syphilis, HIV)
  • Vaccination review
  • Mental health screening

Required Vaccinations

  • MMR (measles, mumps, rubella)
  • Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis)
  • Varicella (chickenpox)
  • Influenza (seasonal)
  • COVID-19
  • Hepatitis A and B

Cost ranges from $200-$350. Results are provided in a sealed envelope — do not open it.

Interview Tips for Kyiv

  • Do not arrive more than 15 minutes early. The Kyiv embassy specifically requests this.
  • Bring all originals. Original documents and copies are both required. Originals (except the medical report) will be returned after the interview.
  • Certified translations. Documents not in English or Ukrainian must have certified English translations.
  • Relationship evidence. Bring extensive proof of your genuine relationship: photos, communication records, travel documentation, and letters from people who know you as a couple.
  • Know your petitioner's details. Address, employer, salary, family members, daily routine.
  • Be prepared for security questions. Given the geopolitical situation, officers may ask additional questions about background and intentions.
  • Previous marriages matter. Bring original divorce certificates and copies of ALL prior marriage termination documents. This is specifically emphasized by the Kyiv embassy.

Common Issues and Denial Reasons

  • Martial law travel restrictions. Male applicants aged 18-60 may be unable to depart Ukraine even with an approved visa.
  • Document inaccessibility. Applicants from occupied or conflict-affected areas may struggle to obtain documents from local offices.
  • Relationship authenticity concerns. As with all K-1 cases, proving a genuine relationship is essential.
  • Prior visa denials or immigration issues. Disclose all previous visa applications and denials honestly.
  • Financial insufficiency. See our I-134 guide.

After the Visa: What Happens Next

  1. Travel to the U.S. within 6 months (subject to Ukrainian departure requirements).
  2. Marry within 90 days. See our 90-day rule guide.
  3. File Adjustment of Status (I-485, $1,440) after marriage.
  4. Apply for work authorization (EAD) concurrently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Kyiv embassy processing K-1 visas?

Yes. As of October 2024, the U.S. Embassy in Kyiv resumed all immigrant and K visa processing for Ukrainian citizens and residents.

How long does the K-1 process take from Ukraine?

Approximately 15-20 months, though wartime conditions may add unpredictable delays.

Can men leave Ukraine with a K-1 visa?

Martial law restricts most men aged 18-60 from departing. Exemption categories exist but change frequently. Consult Ukrainian legal counsel for current rules.

What police certificate do I need?

A criminal record certificate from the Ministry of Internal Affairs of Ukraine.

Do I need to translate Ukrainian documents?

The Kyiv embassy accepts documents in English and Ukrainian. Only documents in other languages need certified English translations.

What if I cannot obtain documents from my home region?

Displaced Ukrainians may be able to obtain documents through relocated government offices or alternative procedures. Contact the embassy for guidance specific to your situation.
