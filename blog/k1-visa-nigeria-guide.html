K-1 Visa from Nigeria: Complete 2026 Guide

Last updated: March 2026·14 min read

Quick Answer

The K-1 visa process from Nigeria takes 13-18 months and costs $2,700-$4,200 total. All interviews are at the U.S. Consulate in Lagos. Nigerian applicants need a Police Clearance Certificate from the Nigeria Police Force (valid 6 months) and a medical exam. The visa fee must be paid at Guaranty Trust Bank. Note: 2026 policy changes may affect Nigerian immigrant visa issuance — consult an immigration professional for current status.

Nigeria is one of the top African countries for K-1 fiance visa petitions. The large Nigerian-American diaspora, particularly in cities like Houston, Atlanta, New York, and the D.C. metro area, creates many cross-border relationships. The Lagos consulate is the only post in Nigeria that processes immigrant and K visas.

This guide covers the Nigeria-specific K-1 visa process in 2026, including important policy changes that may affect Nigerian applicants. If you haven't confirmed eligibility, start with our K-1 visa requirements guide.

Nigeria K-1 Visas: Current Situation

  • Lagos-only processing. All K-1 interviews happen at the U.S. Consulate General in Lagos. The embassy in Abuja does not process immigrant visas.
  • High scrutiny. The Lagos consulate processes a high volume of visa applications and officers are experienced at identifying inauthentic relationships.
  • GTBank payment. The visa fee must be paid at Guaranty Trust Bank PLC branches in Nigeria.
  • 2025 DS-160 requirement. Starting April 2025, the DS-160 confirmation number must start with AA00 and match the one used to make the appointment.
  • 2026 policy changes. Presidential Proclamation 10998 may affect Nigerian immigrant visa applicants (see below).

2026 Policy Changes Affecting Nigerian Applicants

Presidential Proclamation 10998, effective January 2026, paused or limited certain immigrant visa issuances for nationals of several countries, including Nigeria. Key points:

  • Applicants may still submit visa applications and attend scheduled interviews.
  • The consulate continues to schedule appointments for visa interviews.
  • However, applicants may be ineligible for actual visa issuance or admission under the current proclamation.
  • This policy situation is evolving and may change. Consult with an immigration attorney for the most current status.

U.S. Consulate in Lagos

All K-1 visa interviews are at the U.S. Consulate General in Lagos.

Process

  1. Case arrives from NVC.
  2. Pay the $265 visa fee at any Guaranty Trust Bank PLC branch. Keep the receipt number.
  3. Schedule interview at usvisaappt.com using the payment receipt number.
  4. Complete DS-160. Ensure the confirmation number starts with AA00 and matches the appointment.
  5. Get police clearance and medical exam at least 2 weeks before interview.
  6. Interview at Lagos consulate.

Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)

The Nigerian Police Clearance Certificate is issued by the Nigeria Police Force (NPF).

How to Get It

  1. Apply online through the Nigeria Police POSSAP portal (possap.gov.ng) or in person at the Criminal Registry Office.
  2. Provide fingerprints and personal information.
  3. Processing time: 2-4 weeks through POSSAP; same-day possible at Criminal Registry Office in Lagos (depending on volume).
  4. Cost: NGN 5,000-10,000 (~$3-$7).
  5. Validity: 6 months from date of issuance.

The consulate recommends completing the police certificate 2 weeks before your scheduled interview.

Medical Examination

Complete the medical exam with a consulate-designated panel physician. The consulate provides the list of authorized physicians.

Cost: approximately $200-$350 depending on vaccinations. Results come in a sealed envelope — do NOT open it.

Interview Tips for Lagos

  • DS-160 must match. Confirmation number must start with AA00 and match the appointment booking.
  • Bring ALL required original documents. Missing documents will result in rescheduling.
  • I-134 and IRS Tax Return Transcript are mandatory. Financial documentation is heavily scrutinized.
  • Relationship evidence is critical. Bring photos, communication records, travel records, and testimony from people who know your relationship.
  • Be genuine and specific. Lagos officers process many cases and can identify rehearsed or vague answers.
  • Know your petitioner's details. Address, employer, salary, family, daily routine.

Common Issues

  • 2026 proclamation uncertainty. Policy changes may delay or prevent visa issuance even after approval.
  • Relationship authenticity. The number one concern at any K-1 interview.
  • Public charge concerns. Strong financial documentation from the U.S. petitioner is essential.
  • Document availability. Some Nigerian documents (birth certificates especially) may require sworn court declarations if originals are unavailable.
  • Financial insufficiency. See our I-134 guide.

After the Visa

  1. Travel to the U.S. within 6 months.
  2. Marry within 90 days. See our 90-day rule guide.
  3. File Adjustment of Status (I-485, $1,440).
  4. Apply for work authorization (EAD).

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the interview?

U.S. Consulate General in Lagos. The Abuja embassy does not process immigrant visas.

How do I pay the visa fee?

At any Guaranty Trust Bank PLC branch in Nigeria. You need the receipt number to schedule the interview.

What police certificate do I need?

A Police Clearance Certificate from the Nigeria Police Force. Apply through POSSAP online or at the Criminal Registry Office. Valid for 6 months.

Will the 2026 proclamation affect my K-1 visa?

Possibly. Consult with an immigration attorney for the most current guidance. Applications and interviews continue, but visa issuance may be affected.

Are Nigerian documents in English?

Many are, which reduces translation costs. Documents not in English still need certified translations.

How long does it take?

Approximately 14-19 months, potentially longer due to current policy uncertainties.
