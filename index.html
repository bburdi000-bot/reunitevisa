Questions We Hear Every Day

Do I need a lawyer for the K-1 visa? +
No. Most K-1 visas are approved without an attorney. Our service includes professional document preparation plus attorney review of your complete petition — giving you the quality of a law firm at a fraction of the cost.
How much does the K-1 visa cost total? +
Government fees total approximately $940 (I-129F: $675, DS-160: $265). Our service starts at $599. Total cost including medical exams, translations, and other expenses typically ranges from $2,000-$4,000. See our full cost breakdown.
How long does the K-1 visa take? +
The current average is 10-14 months from filing to arrival. USCIS processing of the I-129F petition is averaging 5-7 months in 2026, with additional time for NVC transfer and consular processing.
K-1 or CR-1 — which should we choose? +
It depends on your timeline and situation. The K-1 is generally faster if you want to marry in the U.S. The CR-1 is better if you can marry abroad and want your spouse to arrive with a green card and immediate work authorization. Read our full comparison.
What if our petition is denied? +
We offer a money-back guarantee on our service fee if your properly-filed petition is denied due to our error. We also assist with RFE responses and can connect you with our attorney partner for complex situations.
What countries do you work with? +
We support K-1 visa applications from every country. We have specialized expertise with the Philippines, Colombia, Brazil, Mexico, Ukraine, India, Vietnam, and other high-volume K-1 countries.
